## STAGE 1: STOP (Interrupt the Pattern)

**Purpose**: Pause the unconscious loop. Create space between stimulus and response.

### What STOP Addresses

**The Unconscious Loop**:
```
Trigger → Old Narrative → Automatic Reaction → Same Result → Reinforce Narrative
```

**STOP breaks this loop** by inserting **conscious awareness** between trigger and reaction.

### How to Facilitate STOP

1. **Pattern Recognition**: Help user identify the repeating pattern
   - "What keeps happening?"
   - "Where have you seen this before?"
   - "What's familiar about this situation?"

2. **Trigger Identification**: What activates this pattern?
   - Person, event, feeling, thought
   - External circumstance or internal state

3. **Pause Creation**: Interrupt the automatic flow
   - "Before you react, let's examine what's happening"
   - "What would it look like to STOP here?"

### STOP Questions

- **What pattern am I stuck in?** (The loop you're repeating)
- **What triggers this pattern?** (What activates it)
- **What's the cost of this pattern?** (Where does it lead)
- **What would change if I paused here?** (Space for choice)

### STOP Output

Clear statement of:
- **The Pattern**: [Specific repeating behavior/thought/emotion]
- **The Trigger**: [What activates this pattern]
- **The Cost**: [Where this pattern leads - consequences]

## STAGE 2: SUBMIT (Face Truth, Surrender)

**Purpose**: Acknowledge reality without defense. Face the 4 Truths with humility.

### What SUBMIT Addresses

**The Resistance to Truth**:
- Ego defends stories (even destructive ones)
- Facing truth feels like death (of identity, narrative, comfort)
- Submission is NOT weakness - it's **strength to face what is**

**The Alpha knows**: You cannot change what you won't acknowledge.

### The 4 Truths Framework

Every SUBMIT stage addresses 4 Truths:

1. **FACTS**: Objective reality (what actually happened, measurable, observable)
   - No interpretation, no story - just what is

2. **FEELINGS**: Emotional truth (how you actually feel, beneath the story)
   - Not what you "should" feel
   - Raw, honest emotion

3. **FOCUS**: Mental stance (what story are you telling about Facts and Feelings?)
   - The narrative, the interpretation
   - The meaning you've assigned

4. **FRUIT**: Results produced (what outcomes has this Focus generated?)
   - Where has this story led you?
   - What results prove the story true/false?

### SUBMIT Questions

- **FACTS**: What are the undeniable realities of my situation?
- **FEELINGS**: How do I truly feel about these facts? (No "should")
- **FOCUS**: What story have I been telling myself about this?
- **FRUIT**: What results has this story produced in my life?

### SUBMIT Output

Clear statement of:
- **Facts**: [Objective reality, no interpretation]
- **Feelings**: [Raw emotional truth]
- **Focus**: [The story I've been telling]
- **Fruit**: [Results this story has produced]

## STAGE 3: STRUGGLE (Rewrite the Story)

**Purpose**: Challenge old narratives. Write new ones. Fight for better stories.

### What STRUGGLE Addresses

**The Battle for Narrative Control**:
- Old stories don't die easily (they've kept you "safe")

- Struggle is the work of **conscious rewriting**

**This is NOT "positive affirmations"** - it's deep examination of:
- Why did I believe the old story?
- What evidence contradicts it?
- What would a truer story look like?
- Who would I become if I believed the new story?

### Old Narrative Examination

Help user dissect the old story:
1. **Where did this story come from?** (Origin - childhood, trauma, culture)
2. **What did this story protect me from?** (The "safety" it provided)
3. **What is the cost of this story NOW?** (Where it leads today)
4. **What evidence exists AGAINST this story?** (Contradictions, exceptions)

### New Narrative Creation

Help user write a truer story:
1. **What is a more empowering story?** (Not fantasy - grounded in potential)
2. **What evidence SUPPORTS this new story?** (Past wins, strengths, possibilities)
3. **Who do I become if I believe this?** (Identity shift)
4. **What actions emerge from this story?** (Behavior change)

### STRUGGLE Questions

**Old Narrative**:
- What is the old story I've been telling myself?
- Where did this story come from? (Origin)
- What did this story protect me from? (Function)
- What is the cost of believing this story today?

## STAGE 4: STRIKE (Decisive Action from New Narrative)

**Purpose**: Extract precise action from renewed narrative. Move from insight to execution.

### What STRIKE Addresses

**The Gap Between Knowing and Doing**:
- Insight without action = philosophy tourism
- New narrative without behavior change = still living old story
- STRIKE is the **bridge from mental mastery to real-world DOMINION**

**The Strike is NOT**:
- A to-do list (that's Fire Map / War Stack)
- A vague intention ("be better")
- Multiple actions (Strike is SINGULAR, decisive)

**The Strike IS**:
- **One clear action** that embodies the new narrative
- **Immediate** (not "someday" - THIS WEEK)
- **Concrete** (measurable, observable)
- **Identity-congruent** (who you are in new narrative would naturally do this)

### STRIKE Questions

- **What is ONE action that embodies this new narrative?**
- **What would the person in the new story naturally do?**
- **What's the smallest first step that's also meaningful?**
- **When exactly will I take this Strike?** (Day, time, context)
- **What obstacle might prevent this Strike?** (Anticipate resistance)
- **Who is responsible for this Strike?** (Ownership)

### STRIKE Output

Clear statement of:
- **The Strike**: [One decisive action, concrete and immediate]
- **When**: [Specific timing - this week, today if possible]
- **Why This Strike**: [How it embodies new narrative]
- **Anticipated Obstacle**: [What might prevent execution]
- **Commitment**: [Public declaration / accountability]
